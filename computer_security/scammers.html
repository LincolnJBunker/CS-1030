Scammers

What are Scammers?

A scammer can be an individual person or a group of people who try to trick you into giving them your personal information, such as your credit card number, social security number, or other sensitive data.

How do Scammers 'Scam' you?

There are many ways that scammers can try to scam you. Some of the most common ways include:

  • Phishing Emails: These are emails that look like they are from a legitimate company, but they are actually from a scammer. They may ask you to click on a link or download an attachment that contains malware.
  • Phone Calls: Scammers may call you and pretend to be from a legitimate company. They may ask you to provide personal information or payment information.
  • Fake Websites: Scammers may create fake websites that look like legitimate websites. They may ask you to enter your personal information or payment information.
  • Social Media: Scammers may use social media to try to scam you. They may send you messages or friend requests that contain links to fake websites or phishing emails.

How can I prevent an Attack from a Scammer?

Understanding that scammers are out there is a great first step! Here are some other ways to educate and prepare yourself:

  • Never give away any serious personal information, like credit card information, social security number, address, etc. Only do so if you can confrim 100% that it is a trusted source
  • If you ever have questions, ask a family member, work collegue, friend, or even some local companies can confirm whether a message/email could be from a scammer
  • Never click or open any suspicious link, email, image, text, or anything of that matter
  • Ensure that your passwords are considered 'Strong' (They include an uppercase, numbers, and special character)
