Networking Scavenger Hunt

For this scavenger hunt, I wanted to focus on how my smartphone connects to my favorite app, Spotify. I used the following items from the internet for this exercise:

  1. Smartphone
  2. Wi-Fi Router
  3. Internet / ISP
  4. Spotify Servers
I will break down each step in the process and what I was able to discover. Enjoy!

Smartphone

This is the device that I use to open Spotify, play songs, and recieve the music stream

Why it matters

Without my smartphone, I don't have a way to request or play music. I need a device that can access the netework and display Spotify's UI.

Wi-Fi Router

This device is what connects my phone to the internet, via Wi-Fi. It will route my data to the broader network and then retrieve data back from the internet to my smartphone.

Why it matters

Wi-Fi acts as the gateway between my phone and the interent. With out a router, my phone can't reach Spotify's servers

ISP

The ISP is what provides access to the interent, which allows my home network to communicate with external servers like Spotify's.

Why it matters

This is what bridges my local network to the global internet. Without, my Spotify requests couldn't reach the servers, and I wouldn't be able to stream the music I like.

Spotify Servers

These servers store all of Spotify's songs and handle requests from users like me, sending data back to my phone in real time

Why it matters

This is the source of the music. Without the servers, there would be no music to stream!
