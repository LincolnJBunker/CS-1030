What is Sport Climbing?

Sport Climbing is a style of climbing that uses ropes and harnesses, and also a piece of gear called a quickdraw. On a sport route, there are bolts every 5 - 8 feet on the route and then a set of chains at the top. As the climber climbs, he or she will clip a quickdraw into the the bolt, and then to the rope. The climber does this until they reach the top where they will create a simple anchor to be lowered down. A belayer watches the climber, feeds slack through there belay device, and catches the climber if they fall

What Gear will you Need?

Sport climbing requires shoes and chalk, like bouldering. However, you will need a few more items:

  • Harness
  • Belay Device
  • Rope
  • Quickdraws (10 or more)
  • Helmet
